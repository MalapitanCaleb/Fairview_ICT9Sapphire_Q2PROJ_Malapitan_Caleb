Chicken Adobo

Our signiture recipe!


According to several experts in Asian food history, Filipinos were cooking adobo before Spanish colonization.
Cooking with vinegar preserves meat, and is one of the earliest food preservation practices.
This is also true of pork adobo, which is another popular variation of the dish.

INGREDIENTS
Chicken 2 lbs.
Dried bay leaves 3 PCS.
Water 1 1/2 cup
Soy sauce 4 Tbsp.
Garlic, crushed and chopped 5 Cloves
White vinegar 6 Tbsp.
Whole peppercorn 1 Tsp.
Salt 1/4 Tsp.
Sugar 1 Tsp.
Cooking oil 3 Tbsp.

COOKING INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Add the chicken, garlic and soy sauce in a large bowl. Mix around and make sure the Chicken is well coated. Marinate for at least 1 hour Note: The longer the better.
  2. Heat pan and when the pan is hot add cooking oil
  3. Pan fry the marinated chicken.
  4. Pour the extra marinade and garlic and bring to a boil.
  5. Add dried bay leaves and whole peppercorn. Simmer for 30 mins.r
  6. Add the vinegar and stir for 10 mins
  7. Put-in the sugar, and salt. Stir and turn the heat off. Serve hot. Share and Enjoy!
