Sinigang


Sinigang is a sour soup native to the Philippines. This recipe uses pork as the main ingredient.
Other proteins and seafood can also be used. Beef, shrimp, fish are commonly used to cook sinigang.

INGREDIENTS
Pork Belly 2 lbs.
Young Tamarind 1 lbs.
Water Spinach 1 bunch
String beans 8 pieces(cut into 2 inch pieces)
Eggplants 2 PCS.
Daikon Radish 1 PCS.
Okra 8 PCS.
Long Green Pepper 2 PCS.
Tomato 2 PCS.
Onion 1 PCS.
Water 2 Quart
Patis and black pepper To taste

COOKING INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Simmer the young tamarind in 2 quarts of water for 40 minutes. Strain the tamarind broth using a sieve or strainer, then press the tamarind to extract any remaining juice.
  2. Transfer the tamarind broth to a cooking pot, bring it to a boil, and add the onion, pork belly, and half of the tomatoes.
  3. Remove any floating impurities, stir in 1 tablespoon of fish sauce, cover, and let it simmer for 1 hour.
  4. Mix in the daikon radish and eggplants, cooking them for 5 minutes.
  5. Add the long green pepper, string beans, the rest of the tomatoes, and okra. Cook for another 3 minutes.
  6. Incorporate the chopped water spinach stalks, season with fish sauce and ground black pepper, and cook for 2 minutes.
  7. Place the water spinach leaves on top, cover the pot, and turn off the heat. Allow the residual heat to cook the leaves for 3 minutes before serving.
  8. Serve and enjoy!
